Brikena Hoxha

Brikena Hoxha is a senior attorney with Frost & Fire Consulting. Educated as a lawyer in New York and Tirana, she has significant legal experience in the private and public sector in the U.S.A., Albania and the wider region.

Ms. Hoxha has practiced law in New York for almost a decade as a staff attorney with several prestigious law firms such as Skadden, Arps, Slate, Meagher & Flom LLP, Paul, Weiss, Rifkind, Wharton & Garrison LLP and Covington & Burling, LLP.  During this period she has been involved in various complex litigations relating to white collar crime, anti-trust, contracts, securities class actions, the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act, CDOs (collateralized debt obligations) and RMBS (residential mortgage backed securities).  Her responsibilities have included overseeing the work of very large teams of contract attorneys during an important government investigation, drafting memoranda to highlight issues requiring client input and generally assisting with the document discovery of complex commercial litigation and regulatory matters.  In addition, Ms. Hoxha has represented pro-bono many low income clients in landlord-tenant, child custody and political asylum cases.

Upon her return to Albania and prior to joining Frost & Fire Consulting, Ms. Hoxha was a project based legal consultant for various international organizations such as the United Nations and Development Program (UNDP), the Organization for Security and Cooperation in Europe (OSCE), German International Cooperation (GIZ/GTZ), as well as private entities such as the AHT Group or Pohl Consulting & Associates.  Projects have included providing professional assistance on judicial, legislative, legal education and public administration reform; drafting legislation and assisting with the establishment of new agencies in countries in transition and post-conflict societies as well as providing technical assistance to public companies, government institutions and independent agencies in Kosovo and Albania on a variety of areas, including the transposition of European Union acquis in newly drafted legislation, the liquidation and privatization of state-owned enterprises, the property rights of municipalities and individuals in public interest projects, workers' compensation matters and the drafting of contracts for the sale/purchase of public assets or contracts between utility companies and consumers.

Born in Tirana, Albania, Ms. Hoxha has a B.S. in physics and a Law Degree from the University of Tirana.  In 2003 Ms. Hoxha received a Master of Laws Degree (LL.M.) from Columbia Law School of Columbia University in New York where she was a contributor to the Journal of Eastern European Law. 
Ms. Hoxha is admitted to practice in New York and is a member of the New York City Bar Association. Ms. Hoxha may be reached by phone at +355 4 4500 230 or by e-mail at Brikena.Hoxha@ffconsulting.al.
