Indrit Banka

Indrit Banka is a Senior Financial Consultant at Frost & Fire Consulting. An economist by training, for most of his distinguished career Mr. Banka has been involved in the banking industry, both in the private sector as well as in the regulatory and supervisory capacity of the banking system.

In the private sector Mr. Banka has held several roles ranging the entire span of the industry. He started his career as a loan officer and worked his way up to manager, director and otherexecutive functions with ProCredit Bank and NBG Bank. From 2007 to 2015 he held the position of the Director of the Supervision Department with the Bank of Albania, responsible for licensing, regulating and supervising Albania's banking system, including banks and non-banking financial institutions. More recently Mr. Banka also served as the CEO of an alternative investment vehicle, a novel concept for Albanian market, as well as a consultant.

Mr. Banka's responsibilities in the private banking sector have encompassed:

  • Sourcing, analyzing, developing and improving customer relations.
  • Exploring, analyzing and evaluating alternatives for geographic & product expansion.
  • Overseeing human resources management and development, including recruiting, evaluating, training, and promoting staff on all loan-related policies and procedures.
  • Establishing information flow procedures to ensure that all the information related to loans, customers and personnel was distributed effectively to the relevant parties.
  • Establishing the framework and setting parameters of institutional policies and decision-making processes with respect to retail and institutional lending.
  • Supervising the operations of the respective banking units.

Mr. Banka's tenure as the Director of the Supervision Department of the Bank of Albania, coincided with the 2008 financial crisis, the greatest financial crisis of the global banking system since the Great Depression. Nonetheless, under his leadership as well as the leadership of his bank executive colleagues, the Albanian banking system managed the crisis not only without any negative repercussions, but all the while supporting economic growth. In addition, Mr. Banka managed to accomplish and bring to fruition a great number of projects of vital importance for Albania's banking industry, including:

  • The licensing, regulation and supervision of Albania's banking system, including banks and non-banking financial institutions.
  • The successful execution of the strategic direction and operational agenda of the Supervision Department (comprising over 45 professionals), including compliance with the legal and regulatory framework.
  • The preparation of in-depth banking system analysis as well as that of periodic and on demand specific supervision reports addressed to the Governor or the Supervisory Council.
  • Initiating numerous improvements of the supervisory process, undertaking a full reorganization process to successfully implement risk- focus supervision, enhancing effectiveness and improving efficiency.
  • Establishing a systematic approach for exchanging information and bringing to resolution supervisory issues with the representatives of the banking sector, as well as other local and international counterparts.
  • Initiating revisions of the legal and regulatory framework to better tackle/address the risks/vulnerabilitites of the the stability of the financial sector.
  • Working closely with, and participating in, various technical groups within the Central Bank as well as with partner institutions, such as the Financial Supervisory Authority, the Competition Authority, the Financial Intelligence Unit, the Non-Performing Loans Resolution Group, the Bank Resolution Initiative, the Banks' Automated Reporting System, the Financial Stability Advisory Group etc. in order to institute various improvements in the Deposit Insurance law, Anti Money Laundering law, non performing loans regulatory framework and other crucial elements of the Albanian financial reform efforts.
  • Representing the Bank of Albania in various national and international initiatives and regulatory bodies, such as Vienna Initiative 2 (forum member; coordinator for non EU-member countries supervision), FinSac (helping design several products such as the Whole Portfolio Restructuring Program), FSAP (main country counterpart for the World Bank/IMF), Export Credit Guarantee Fund (member of the Guarantees' Evaluation & Approval Committee), ATK (Agency for the Resolution of Bad Loans) (vice chairman of the Supervisory Board), Credit Lines Steering Committee (member of committee), SME credit lines, BSCEE (member; chairman of the Supervisors Banking Group of CEE), SPI Albania (counterparty for the implementation of the financial sector reforms).

Upon his return in the private sector Mr. Banka's experiences have ranged from being been responsible for the management and direction of a company with over 20 million euros in assets, to performing in depth tailored policy research and risk analysis for local and international organizations (including the World Bank and the Albanian Association of Banks), to advising or spearheading full cycle project management financing (intermediation and negotiations for public and private entities, including with banks and non-banking financial institutions).

Mr. Banka was born in Tirana, Albania. He received a Business Administration degree from the University of Tirana in 1996. He is married with two sons and is an avid skier. Mr. Banka may be reached by phone at +35544500230 or by e-mail at indrit.banka@ffconsulting.al.
